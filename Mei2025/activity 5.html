Introduction to Famous Sculpture Artists



Michelangelo (1475–1564, Italy)

Michelangelo di Lodovico Buonarroti Simoni was an Italian Renaissance artist and one of the greatest sculptors of all time. Known for his mastery of anatomy and emotional depth, he shaped the course of Western art.

Famous works:

  • David: A marble statue symbolizing human strength and beauty.
  • Pietà: A masterpiece of the Virgin Mary holding the body of Jesus Christ, filled with emotion.
  • Moses: A powerful sculpture of Moses, showing Michelangelo's skill in form.


Auguste Rodin (1840–1917, France)

Rodin is considered the father of modern sculpture. His works capture emotion and realism beyond traditional forms.

Famous works:
  • The Thinker: A man in deep contemplation, symbolizing intellectual struggle.
  • The Kiss: A romantic sculpture of two lovers embracing.
  • The Burghers of Calais: Shows the sacrifice of six citizens during wartime.


Anish Kapoor (1954–present, India/UK)

Anish Kapoor is a British-Indian sculptor known for large-scale, reflective, abstract installations that challenge perception.

Famous works:

  • Cloud Gate (The Bean): A shiny sculpture in Chicago reflecting the skyline and people.
  • Sky Mirror: A concave mirror that reflects the sky and surroundings.
  • Descent into Limbo: A dark pit artwork symbolizing emptiness and the unknown.
